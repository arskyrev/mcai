

Overall design:


A large, multi-story medieval-style building
Primarily constructed of dark oak wood and stone bricks
Asymmetrical layout with multiple sections and roof levels


Exterior features:


Three-story central structure with two smaller two-story wings
Steep, overhanging roofs made of dark oak stairs and slabs
Multiple dormer windows on each roof level
Stone brick foundation and first floor
Upper floors made of dark oak planks and logs
Decorative support beams and crossbeams visible on exterior


Windows:


Large, arched windows on the first and second floors
Smaller, square windows on the top floor
Windows framed with dark oak stairs and slabs
Some windows have pink or purple stained glass


Entrance:


Grand arched entryway on the first floor, centrally located
Dark oak door with stone brick frame


Roof details:


Multiple peaked roofs with varying heights
Overhanging eaves with exposed beam ends
Small balconies or terraces on some roof sections
Chimney-like structures at roof peaks


Interior (based on visible portions):


Open floor plan with high ceilings
Exposed wooden beams and supports
Stone brick walls with some sections of bare wood
Multiple floors connected by staircases


Storage and decoration:


Wooden crates and barrels scattered throughout
Bookshelves along walls
Hanging lanterns for lighting
Wooden fences used as railings and room dividers


Color scheme:


Dark brown (dark oak) for most wooden elements
Light gray (stone bricks) for foundations and some walls
Accents of pink and purple from stained glass

Ensure the structure has a cohesive medieval tavern or inn aesthetic, with attention to detail in both the exterior architecture and interior design elements visible through windows and doorways.
